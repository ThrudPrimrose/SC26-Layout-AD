\section{Overview of Contributions and Artifacts}

\subsection{Paper's Main Contributions}

\begin{description}
\item[$C_1$] A theoretical proof, via blocked CDAGs and the Red-Blue-White Pebble Game, that a poor data layout can inflate the I/O lower bound by up to a factor of the cache-line length $B$ relative to a layout-aware optimum, and that no schedule can close this gap.
\item[$C_2$] A composable layout algebra of five primitives (pad, permute, block, shuffle, zip) whose compositions express any bijective array rearrangement.
\item[$C_3$] Two heuristic cost metrics --- the average new-block count $\mu$ (guiding permutation) and the average block distance $\Delta$ (guiding blocking) --- with a cookbook (Table~II) mapping observable performance symptoms to corrective primitives.
\item[$C_4$] Empirical validation on microbenchmarks and two production HPC codes (Quantum ESPRESSO, ICON dynamical core) showing up to $1.18\times$ speedup on GPUs and $1.12\times$ on CPUs.
\end{description}

\section{Artifact Identification}

\newartifact

\artrel

Artifact~$A_1$ validates the layout algebra ($C_2$), cost metrics ($C_3$), and the cookbook-driven workflow ($C_4$). E1--E4 are microbenchmarks isolating individual primitives. E5 evaluates composed transformations on a Quantum ESPRESSO production kernel. E6 applies the full optimization workflow per loop-nest pattern class. E8 drives the resulting layouts through the velocity-tendencies module end-to-end on the GPU.

\artexp

\textbf{E1 (Matrix Addition, Fig.~4):} elementwise $C \mathrel{+}= A + B$ with $B$ in column-major. Schedule-only recovers 36\%--72\% of STREAM peak on CPUs and 78\%--95\% on GPUs; layout transformations close the gap to 68\%--88\% and 99\%, respectively.

\textbf{E2 (Complex Conjugation, Fig.~8):} in-place conjugation of $P$ complex arrays under AoS, SoA, AoSoA-16. SoA collapses on Zen4 at high $P$ from L1D set conflicts; AoSoA-16 stays robust across all four backends.

\textbf{E3 (Matrix Transpose, Fig.~9, Tab.~III):} $N \times N$ fp32 transpose, row-major vs.\ blocked. Blocking gains $1.7\times$ on Zen4 and $3.2\times$ on Grace, reaching 61\%--81\% of STREAM peak vs.\ HPTT's 44\%. On GPUs, shared-memory tiling absorbs the stride.

\textbf{E4 (Gather-Accumulate-Scatter, Fig.~10):} indirect $y[\sigma(i)] \mathrel{+}= x[i]$ with vs.\ without index-sort shuffle. GPU bandwidth improves 21\%$\to$30\% (uniform) and 23\%$\to$42\% (BaTiO$_3$) on MI300A; 35\%$\to$62\% and 48\%$\to$82\% on Hopper. CPU gains 9\%--15\%.

\textbf{E5 (QE \texttt{addusxx\_g}, Fig.~11):} composed unzip + permute + shuffle. Speedups: $1.12\times$ Zen4, $1.11\times$ Grace, $1.18\times$ Hopper, $1.00\times$ MI300A.

\textbf{E6 (ICON velocity tendencies, Figs.~12--13, Tab.~IV):} the module's 25 loop nests / 50 arrays collapse into 6 pattern classes and 7 layout-equivalent groups. Per-class representative loop nests are evaluated as separate microbenchmarks (\texttt{loopnest\_\{1..6\}/}) sweeping V1--V7 dimension orderings over $nlev \in \{90, 96, 128, 256\}$ (nlev=128 is the default for Tab.~IV; Fig.~12 violins use nlev=96). Loopnest~1 (indirect stencil, complete vertical) gains $1.85\times$ on Zen4 and large factors on GPUs under uniform random indirection; under exact R02B05 connectivity GPUs are comparable across layouts (Tab.~IV reports $\mu$ for both regimes).

\textbf{E8 (Full velocity tendencies, Fig.~14, Tab.~V):} GPU layout-permutation sweep over the full module via \texttt{run\_stage8\_permutations.py} on the icon-artifacts stage~8 SDFGs. Default \texttt{CONFIGS="winner\_v1,winner\_v2,winner\_v6"}; the 95-cell cross-product (\texttt{cv*\_ch*\_f*\_s*\_n*}) is opt-in. Vertical-first yields $1.17\times$ on MI300A and $1.07\times$ on Hopper for Config~B (5 of every 6 calls), $1.06\times$ / $0.97\times$ for Config~A. Net per-timestep: $\sim$15\% MI300A, $\sim$5\% Hopper (5:1 weighted).

\paragraph{Reproducibility caveat.} \%-of-STREAM annotations may differ slightly from the paper's. The cache-flush kernel was rewritten as a 3-sweep $8192^{2}$ Jacobi with buffer swap to defeat dead-code-elimination by the GPU compiler, and the MI300A STREAM Triad peak was independently cross-checked with AMD performance engineering; every figure in this artifact is normalized to the corrected peak. \artin

\artinpart{Hardware}

Two HPC clusters at the Swiss National Supercomputing Centre (CSCS), accessed via SLURM with exclusive single-node allocations:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Daint.Alps:} Quad-GH200 nodes, 72-core Arm Neoverse~V2 Grace (512\,GB/s LPDDR5X) + NVIDIA H200 (4\,TB/s HBM3). Measured STREAM Triad peaks: CPU 1.81\,TB/s, GPU 3.78\,TB/s.
\item \textbf{Beverin:} Quad-MI300A APU nodes, 24 AMD Zen\,4 cores sharing 128\,GB unified HBM3. Measured STREAM Triad peaks: CPU 1.16\,TB/s, GPU 3.55\,TB/s (cross-checked with AMD performance engineering).
\end{itemize}

Results should generalize to any system with comparable cache-line sizes (64\,B CPU, 128\,B GPU); absolute bandwidths will differ.

\paragraph{CUDA pin (Daint).} \textbf{CUDA must be \textless{}~13.} CUDA~13 removes \texttt{cudaDeviceProp::clockRate}, which DaCe's runtime probes during initialization, breaking codegen for E8's stage-8 binaries. CUDA~12.9 is the verified version.

\paragraph{OpenBLAS version split.} Daint and Beverin pin different OpenBLAS minor versions (0.3.29 vs.\ 0.3.30) because the Beverin Spack instance's \texttt{openblas@0.3.29} was uninstalled mid-cycle while Daint's persisted; both setup scripts probe their respective Spack and skip the OpenBLAS \texttt{LD\_LIBRARY\_PATH} export gracefully if neither version is present (used only by E3's CPU baseline). The version difference does not affect E1--E2, E4--E8 measurements.

\paragraph{Compile flags.} The setup scripts export \texttt{CPU\_CXXFLAGS} and \texttt{GPU\_CXXFLAGS}; every ad-hoc \texttt{g++}/\texttt{nvcc}/\texttt{hipcc} invocation in the Python drivers carries the same set:

\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{CPU\_CXXFLAGS} (both platforms): \texttt{-O3 -march=native -mtune=native -fopenmp -ffast-math -fno-trapping-math -fno-math-errno -fno-vect-cost-model -std=c++17}
\item \texttt{GPU\_CXXFLAGS} (Daint, nvcc): \texttt{-O3 --use\_fast\_math -arch=sm\_90 -std=c++17} plus the CPU set forwarded to the host GCC pass via \texttt{-Xcompiler=}.
\item \texttt{GPU\_CXXFLAGS} (Beverin, hipcc): the CPU set minus \texttt{-fno-vect-cost-model} (Clang has no equivalent), plus \texttt{--offload-arch=gfx942 -munsafe-fp-atomics -mllvm -amdgpu-early-inline-all=true -mllvm -amdgpu-function-calls=false -fgpu-flush-denormals-to-zero}.
\end{itemize}

\texttt{-fno-trapping-math} and \texttt{-fno-math-errno} are already implied by \texttt{-ffast-math}; they are listed explicitly so intent survives any future \texttt{-ffast-math} definition drift.

\paragraph{OpenMP and pinning.} \texttt{OMP\_SCHEDULE=static} is exported by both setup scripts; every \texttt{\#pragma omp parallel for} in our sources uses \texttt{schedule(static)}. CPU-to-core pinning via \texttt{SLURM\_CPU\_BIND=cores} (no \texttt{\#SBATCH --cpu-bind}).

\artinpart{Installation and Deployment}

Each experiment ships \texttt{run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh}, which sources \texttt{../common/activate.sh} (venv + DaCe pin) and \texttt{../common/setup\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh} (Spack loads, OMP/NUMA pinning, \texttt{CPU\_CXX}/\texttt{GPU\_CXX} flags), then compiles, runs benchmarks (100~reps after 5~warm-ups with cache flushes), and writes results next to each experiment.
One-time per cluster: \texttt{bash Experiments/common/setup.sh}; per experiment: \texttt{cd Experiments/EX\_\textit{name} \&\& sbatch run\_daint.sh}; plot: \texttt{python plot\_paper.py} (or \texttt{bash Figures/plot\_all.sh Runtime} for everything at once).

\artcomp

E1--E5 each follow a four-task workflow encapsulated in \texttt{run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh}: $T_1$~setup (sources shared env; auto-fetch dataset for E4/E5/E6/E8), $T_2$~build and execute (100~reps after 5~warm-ups with cache flushes), $T_3$~compute cost metrics (E3 only; $\mu$ and $\Delta$), $T_4$~plot (\texttt{plot\_paper.py}). Dependencies: $T_1\to T_2$, $T_1\to T_3$, $\{T_2,T_3\}\to T_4$. The run script handles $T_1$--$T_3$; $T_4$ is invoked manually. E1--E5 are mutually independent.

\textbf{E6 workflow.} Multi-stage, mirroring §III-F:
\begin{itemize}
\item $T_{6.1}$ data fetch (auto-invoked).
\item $T_{6.2}$ LOKI access analysis $\to$ 25 nests $\to$ 6 representative nests, \texttt{layout\_candidates.json}.
\item $T_{6.3}$ canonical 6-group taxonomy (\texttt{cv}, \texttt{ch}, \texttt{f}, \texttt{s}, \texttt{n}, \texttt{lm}) $\to$ \texttt{canonical\_array\_groups.json}.
\item $T_{6.4}$ per-loopnest evaluation under \texttt{loopnest\_\{1..6\}/}; sweeps V1--V7 plus blocked factors over $nlev \in \{90, 96, 128, 256\}$.
\item $T_{6.5}$ winners cross-product: \texttt{generate\_winners.py}, \texttt{derive\_winners.py}, \texttt{generate\_layout\_groups.py} produce \texttt{winners.json} and the §IV-A 7-group spec \texttt{layout\_groups.json}.
\item $T_{6.6}$ analysis: \texttt{plot\_paper.py} $\to$ Figs.~12--13, Tab.~IV.
\end{itemize}

Dependencies $T_{6.1}\to\dots\to T_{6.6}$. \texttt{winners.json} and \texttt{layout\_groups.json} feed E8.

\textbf{E8 workflow.} A single \texttt{sbatch run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh} resolves the dataset (existing E8 dir $\to$ E7 symlink $\to$ fresh fetch), regenerates \texttt{winners.json} / \texttt{layout\_candidates.json} if missing, then dispatches \texttt{run\_stage8\_permutations.py} with the \texttt{CONFIGS} env (default \texttt{winner\_v1,winner\_v2,winner\_v6}). For each named config, \texttt{compile\_gpu\_stage8} produces shuffled (\texttt{\_ms}) and unshuffled (\texttt{\_mu}) binaries; stdout is captured under \texttt{<plat>\_full\_permutations\_8/} as one TXT per binary. Plotting via \texttt{Figures/plot\_paper\_snapshot.sh} or \texttt{plot\_results.sh}; both auto-detect E8's TXT layout.

\textbf{Statistical methodology.} Following Hoefler and Belli, every configuration runs 100~reps after 5~warm-ups. An $8192^2$ Jacobi stencil (3~sweeps with buffer swap; \texttt{common/jacobi\_flush.h}) flushes all cache levels between reps. We report medians with 95\% bootstrap CIs of the median (BCa, $n=10{,}000$ resamples). Violin plots show all 100~samples (no outlier trimming); KDE uses Scott's rule and 200 eval points, identical across every figure. RNG seed: \texttt{SC26\_SEED=42}. Bandwidths are normalized to the measured STREAM Triad peak per platform.
