\section{Overview of Contributions and Artifacts}

\subsection{Paper's Main Contributions}

\begin{description}
\item[$C_1$] A theoretical proof, via blocked CDAGs and the Red-Blue-White Pebble Game, that a poor data layout can inflate the I/O lower bound by up to a factor of the cache-line length~$B$ relative to a layout-aware optimum, and that no schedule can close this gap.
\item[$C_2$] A composable layout algebra of five primitives (pad, permute, block, shuffle, zip) whose compositions suffice to express any bijective array rearrangement.
\item[$C_3$] Two heuristic cost metrics---the average new-block count~$\mu$ (guiding permutation) and the average block distance~$\Delta$ (guiding blocking)---together with a cookbook (Table~II) that maps observable performance symptoms to corrective primitives.
\item[$C_4$] Empirical validation on microbenchmarks and two production HPC codes---Quantum ESPRESSO and the ICON dynamical core (2025 ACM Gordon Bell Prize for Weather and Climate Modelling winner)---demonstrating up to $1.18\times$ speedup on GPUs and $1.12\times$ on CPUs.
\end{description}

\section{Artifact Identification}

\newartifact

\artrel

Artifact~$A_1$ validates the layout algebra~($C_2$), cost metrics~($C_3$), and the cookbook-driven optimization workflow~($C_4$). E1--E4 are microbenchmarks that isolate individual layout primitives. E5 evaluates composed transformations on a Quantum ESPRESSO production kernel. E6 applies the full optimization workflow to the ICON dynamical core (loop-nest canonicalization, per-pattern layout selection, cross-nest conflict resolution); E7 then drives the resulting layouts through the velocity-tendencies module end-to-end on the GPU.

\artexp

\textbf{E1 (Matrix Addition, Figure~4):}
Elementwise $C \mathrel{+}= A + B$ with $B$ in column-major. Schedule-only optimization recovers 36\% (Zen4) to 72\% (Grace) of STREAM peak on CPUs and 78\% (MI300A) to 95\% (Hopper) on GPUs; layout transformations close the gap to 68\% / 88\% on CPUs and 99\% on both GPUs. On platforms with explicit scratchpad memory, schedule-only tiling into shared memory should recover most of peak; on CPUs without direct cache control, a persistent gap remains that only layout transformations can close.

\textbf{E2 (Complex Conjugation, Figure~8):}
In-place conjugation of $P$ complex arrays under AoS, SoA, and AoSoA-16. SoA should beat AoS at low~$P$ (fewer wasted cache-line bytes), but on CPUs with limited L1D associativity it collapses at high~$P$ from set conflicts on page-aligned allocations --- observed on Zen4 at $P \geq 15$, not on Grace. Both GPUs sustain near-peak under SoA and AoSoA-16; AoS strides through non-contiguous fields. AoSoA-16 stays robust across all four backends and every~$P$, showing the value of partial zipping when the optimal granularity is not statically selectable.

\textbf{E3 (Matrix Transpose, Figure~9, Table~III):}
$N \times N$ fp32 transpose, row-major vs.\ blocked. On CPUs, row-major writes scatter at stride~$N$ past prefetcher/TLB capacity; blocking gains $1.7\times$ on Zen4 and $3.2\times$ on Grace, reaching 61\% / 81\% of STREAM peak vs.\ HPTT's 44\%. On GPUs, shared-memory tiling absorbs the stride: 60\% (MI300A) and 85\% (Hopper) under row-major, matching vendor libraries (padding or shuffling still needed to avoid bank conflicts). Table~III reports the $\Delta$ reduction from blocking at each cache-line size. Blocking is the primary CPU recourse for stride-heavy access; on GPUs the penalty is absorbed by shared-memory staging.

\textbf{E4 (Gather-Accumulate-Scatter, Figure~10):}
Indirect $y[\sigma(i)] \mathrel{+}= x[i]$, before and after a shuffle (index sorting), under uniform-random and exact BaTiO$_3$ index distributions. The shuffle improves GPU bandwidth from 21\% to 30\% (uniform) / 23\% to 42\% (BaTiO$_3$) on MI300A, and 35\% to 62\% / 48\% to 82\% on Hopper. CPU gains are modest --- 9--15\% Zen4, 7--13\% Grace --- because the shuffle introduces a new indirect gather that limits prefetcher effectiveness. GPU gains generalize to any coalescing-sensitive architecture; CPU gains depend on the trade-off between improved scatter locality and degraded gather locality.

\textbf{E5 (Quantum ESPRESSO \texttt{addusxx\_g}, Figure~11):}
Composed unzip, permute, and shuffle on \texttt{addusxx\_g}. Speedups: $1.12\times$ Zen4, $1.11\times$ Grace, $1.18\times$ Hopper, $1.00\times$ MI300A. The improvement generalizes wherever the baseline Fortran AoS wastes cache-line bandwidth on the untouched real part (unzip), misaligns the innermost stride (permute), and scatters indirect writes (shuffle); MI300A shows no gain because its unified memory absorbs the access pattern.

\textbf{E6 (ICON Velocity Tendencies, Figures~12--13, Table~IV):}
The velocity-tendencies module's 25~loop nests / 50~arrays collapse into 6~pattern classes with 7~layout-equivalent array groups after canonicalization. E6 evaluates a representative loop nest from each class:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Loopnest~1 (indirect stencil, complete vertical):} Vertical-first layout improves CPU bandwidth by $1.85\times$ on Zen4 (30\% $\to$ 55\% of STREAM peak), $1.07\times$ on Grace (44\% $\to$ 47\%). GPUs are comparable under exact R02B05 connectivity (48--49\% MI300A, 90--91\% Hopper); under uniform random indirection vertical-first wins large ($3.3\times$ MI300A, $2.3\times$ Hopper). $\mu$ is invariant to the indirection distribution under vertical-first (Table~IV).
\item \emph{Loopnests~2--6:} Representative nests from the remaining five classes (direct stencil / partial vertical; direct stencil / complete vertical; indirect stencil + CFL clip / partial vertical; horizontal-only boundary; vertical-only level reduction) --- each a C++/OpenMP + CUDA + HIP suite plus an analytic cost-metric sweep over V1--V4 dimension orderings, $B \in \{8,16,32,64,128\}$ blocking, $(T_X, T_Y) \in \{8,16,32,64\}^2$ tiling (where vertical locality is meaningful), and GPU thread-block sizes. The analytic metrics mirror the sweep axes and let us cross-check empirical ordering against~$\mu$/$\Delta$.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{E7 (Full velocity-tendencies module, Figure~14, Table~V):}
End-to-end GPU layout-permutation sweep over the full velocity-tendencies module on the stage~5 SDFGs that ship with the AD. The default flow runs the three named heuristic configs from \texttt{utils.passes.permute\_configs.configs\_from\_candidates} (\texttt{unpermuted}, \texttt{nlev\_first}, \texttt{index\_only}); reviewers can opt into the per-nest \emph{listed} configs or the 94-cell \emph{curated} sweep by passing \texttt{CONFIGS=...}. Vertical-first yields $1.17\times$ on MI300A and $1.07\times$ on Hopper for Config~B (5 of every 6 calls); Config~A shows $1.06\times$ on MI300A and $0.97\times$ on Hopper. Net per-timestep improvement: $\sim$15\% on MI300A, $\sim$5\% on Hopper (5:1 weighted).

The artifact is organized as one folder per experiment.
Experiments E1--E5 each follow the same four-task workflow, encapsulated in their respective \texttt{run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh} scripts:

\begin{description}
\item[$T_1$] \textbf{Setup:} source shared env and install DaCe. Datasets are auto-fetched by the \texttt{run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh} guard on first submission (E4, E5, E6, E7).
\item[$T_2$] \textbf{Build and execute:} compile sources and run benchmarks (100~repetitions after 5~warm-up iterations with cache flushes between repetitions).
\item[$T_3$] \textbf{Compute cost metrics} (E3 only): run \texttt{cost\_metrics} to compute $\mu$ and~$\Delta$ for each layout candidate.
\item[$T_4$] \textbf{Generate plots:} run \texttt{plot\_paper.py} to produce violin plots from the CSV results.

\textbf{E6 (ICON) workflow.}
Experiment~E6 follows a multi-stage pipeline that mirrors the paper's optimization methodology (Section~III-F):

\begin{description}
\item[$T_{6.1}$] \textbf{Download data:} the per-loopnest \texttt{loopnest\_1/download\_data.sh} fetches the R02B05 dataset (auto-invoked by \texttt{run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh}; idempotent).

\item[$T_{6.2}$] \textbf{Access analysis:} \texttt{access\_analysis/analyze\_access.py} (plus \texttt{select\_loopnests.py}, \texttt{jaccard.py}, \texttt{coaccess.py}) canonicalizes the 25~loop nests into 6~pattern classes, groups the 50~arrays into 7~layout-equivalent groups, and computes $\mu$/$\Delta$ for each candidate layout per group.
Outputs the candidate configuration file \texttt{layout\_candidates.json}.

\item[$T_{6.3}$] \textbf{Per-loopnest evaluation:} each \texttt{loopnest\_\{1..6\}/} folder is a full benchmark suite that sweeps V1--V4 dimension orderings, 1D blocking, and (where meaningful) 2D tiling.
Each folder's \texttt{run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh} compiles, runs, and writes CSVs to \texttt{results/\{daint,beverin\}/}; the per-folder \texttt{README.md} explains the Fortran reference nest and the mapping between the code's macros and the nest class.

\item[$T_{6.4}$] \textbf{Conflict resolution:} the per-loopnest rankings from $T_{6.3}$ are combined offline by weighting each loopnest by its call frequency (Config~A: 1/6, Config~B: 5/6); the resulting cross-product of locally-optimal layouts is documented in \texttt{conflict\_resolution/README.md} and consumed by E7 alongside \texttt{layout\_candidates.json}.

\item[$T_{6.5}$] \textbf{Analysis:} the per-loopnest plot scripts under \texttt{loopnest\_\{1..6\}/plot\_paper.py} produce Figures~12--13 and Table~IV from the combined results of $T_{6.3}$.
\end{description}

\noindent Dependencies: $T_{6.1} \to T_{6.2} \to T_{6.3} \to T_{6.4} \to T_{6.5}$. The full-module sweep is a separate experiment, E7 (below).

\textbf{E6 sub-experiment structure.}
\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{access\_analysis/} ($T_{6.2}$): parses the annotated \texttt{velocity\_advection.f90}, canonicalizes the 25~loop nests / 50~arrays into 6~pattern classes / 7~layout-equivalent groups, emits \texttt{layout\_candidates.json}.
\item \texttt{loopnest\_\{1..6\}/} ($T_{6.3}$): one subfolder per pattern class, each a C++/OpenMP + CUDA + HIP suite with analytic cost metrics. Classes: (1) indirect stencil / complete vertical; (2) direct stencil / partial vertical; (3) direct stencil / complete vertical; (4) indirect stencil + CFL clip / partial vertical; (5) horizontal-only boundary; (6) vertical-only level reduction.

\subsubsection*{E7 --- Full velocity tendencies (full-module GPU permutation sweep)}

E7 drives a GPU layout-permutation sweep over the full velocity-tendencies module on the stage~5 SDFGs that ship with the AD, using E6's access-analysis output ($T_{6.2}$) to derive its configs. E7 is \emph{self-contained} --- it vendors its own \texttt{utils/}, \texttt{src/}, etc. from \texttt{VelocityTendenciesPipeline}, so reviewers do not need a sibling checkout.

\begin{description}
\item[$T_{7.1}$] \textbf{Data fetch:} \texttt{tools/download\_data.sh} fetches the R02B05 / \texttt{nproma=20480} ICON dataset ($\sim$9~GB) from ETH PolyBox into \texttt{data\_r02b05/}. Auto-invoked by \texttt{run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh} on first submission and idempotent; \texttt{URL} / \texttt{EXPECTED\_SHA256} support mirror fallback.

\item[$T_{7.2}$] \textbf{Stage 6 sweep:} \texttt{sbatch run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh}. The static drivers follow the E1--E6 convention (source \texttt{../../common/\{activate,setup\_<host>\}.sh}) and iterate over the \texttt{CONFIGS} env var (default \texttt{"unpermuted nlev\_first index\_only"}), running \texttt{python -m utils.stages.stage6 --optimize --compile --config <name>} per cell. Configs are dispatched through \texttt{extended\_configs\_from\_candidates}: \emph{named} (\texttt{unpermuted}, \texttt{nlev\_first}, \texttt{index\_only}); \emph{listed} (one \texttt{nest\_<nid>\_<shape>} per \texttt{all\_nests[]} entry of shape \texttt{h} or \texttt{v.h}); \emph{curated} (the 94-cell port of icon-artifacts' \texttt{sc26\_layout/permute\_stage4.py}, plus \texttt{curated\_nlev\_first} / \texttt{curated\_index\_only}). \texttt{\_FORCE\_PERMUTED} overrides are merged into every config; the canonical entry is \texttt{levmask}, transposed to restore Fortran-canonical \texttt{(nlev, nblks\_c)} order. Per-config CSVs land in \texttt{results/<platform>/<config>/}. \texttt{python tools/list\_layout\_configs.py} prints the available config names.

\item[$T_{7.3}$] \textbf{Optional baseline regeneration:} \texttt{tools/regenerate\_baselines.sh} runs F90 $\to$ stage~5 entirely inside E7 (phase~0: f2dace on \texttt{baseline\_inputs/velocity\_modified.f90}; phase~1: \texttt{generate\_baselines.py}; phases~2--6: \texttt{python -m utils.stages.stage\{1..5\} --optimize}). Phase~0 needs DaCe on \texttt{f2dace/staging}; the rest on \texttt{yakup/dev}. Branch switching is the reviewer's responsibility.

\item[$T_{7.4}$] \textbf{Analysis:} \texttt{Figures/plot\_results.sh} (and the paper-canonical sibling \texttt{Figures/plot\_paper\_snapshot.sh}) produce Figure~14 and Table~V from the per-config CSVs.
\end{description}

\noindent Dependencies: $T_{7.1} \to T_{7.2} \to T_{7.4}$. The opt-in $T_{7.3}$ runs before $T_{7.2}$ when the reviewer wants to rebuild stage~5 from F90 instead of using the shipped SDFGs.

\textbf{E7 directory structure.} Mirrors \texttt{VelocityTendenciesPipeline} (\texttt{baseline\_inputs/}, \texttt{baseline/}, \texttt{SDFGs/stage5/}, \texttt{codegen/stage\{1..6\}/}, \texttt{utils/}, \texttt{src/}, \texttt{include/}, \texttt{tests/}, \texttt{generate\_baselines.py}, \texttt{compile\_baselines.py}, \texttt{main.cpp}) plus static \texttt{run\_\{daint,beverin\}.sh} and three E7-specific helpers under \texttt{tools/} (\texttt{download\_data.sh}, \texttt{regenerate\_baselines.sh}, \texttt{list\_layout\_configs.py}).

\textbf{Statistical methodology.}
Following Hoefler and Belli~[52], every configuration runs 100~reps after 5~warm-ups. An $8192^2$ Jacobi stencil (3~sweeps with buffer swap; \texttt{common/jacobi\_flush.h}) flushes all cache levels between reps. We report medians with 95\% bootstrap CIs of the median (BCa, $n=10{,}000$ resamples). Violin plots show all 100~samples --- no outlier trimming. The KDE uses Scott's rule and 200~equally-spaced eval points, identical across every figure, so violin shapes are directly comparable. All RNG is seeded from \texttt{SC26\_SEED=42} (\texttt{common/prng.h}); bandwidths are normalized to the measured STREAM Triad peak per platform.
